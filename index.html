Abstract

Robust long-horizon robotic manipulation in open environments is a fundamental requirement for deploying robots in daily life. Without a closed loop that integrates perception, reasoning, and execution, robots cannot withstand disturbances and drift away from global goals, hindering practical deployment. Yet existing Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models directly map observations to actions and struggle to generalize to unseen scenes. Hierarchical frameworks rely on lengthy prompt-based spatial inference, which is fragile and prone to hallucination. Moreover, the absence of closed-loop feedback in reasoning prevents online correction, leading to error accumulation that destabilizes long-horizon execution, particularly under disturbances. To address these limitations, we propose Right-in-Left Brain (RiLBrain), a closed-loop framework that integrates spatial perception into reasoning via a unified VLM, reducing error accumulation and enabling online correction in long-horizon tasks. Experiments validate the effectiveness of RiLBrain, showing clear advantages over representative VLAs and hierarchical baselines in long-horizon manipulation with strong robustness and generalization.

Method-Affordance and manipulation constraint prediction

We fine-tune the Qwen3-VL-32B to predict affordances and manipulation constraints, which mitigates hallucination and enables fast and reliable prediction.

Given multi-view images and task instructions, the model outputs structured tuples.

We construct unified supervision labels for affordance and manipulation constraint prediction based on the HANDAL and YCB datasets.

Method-Pose tracking and spatial constraints reasoning

We integrate pose tracking with task-driven spatial reasoning, which provides closed-loop feedback and ensures feasible and plausible poses for objects interaction during execution.

Method-CoT Planning with Online Correction

We design a CoT planner with online correction to maintain global task consistency and robustness under disturbances.

Workflow of CoT planning with online correction. The planner first decomposes the task into an initial subtask pipeline, and then alternates between subtask execution and feedback-driven replanning. Continuously updated perception from the “right brain” provides visual servoing for closed-loop execution, while feedback-based online replanning maintains global task consistency.

Method-Hybrid Execution with Analytic Control and RL

We adopt a hybrid execution strategy with analytic control for simple tasks and reinforcement learning(RL) for complex and dexterous tasks. As analytic control is mature, we primarily detail the RL component, demonstrated on two representative tasks, namely drawer and cabinet opening.

Experimental Setup

We set up a real-world tabletop workspace with two JAKA ZU5 robots (6-DoF), equipped with a BrainCo Hand (11-DoF) and a DH AG95 parallel gripper (1-DoF).
